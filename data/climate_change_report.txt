Climate change refers to long-term changes in global temperatures and weather patterns. While climate variations naturally occur over time, scientific evidence shows that human activities have significantly accelerated the current rate of climate change.

The primary cause of modern climate change is the increase in greenhouse gases in the Earth's atmosphere. These gases, including carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide, trap heat from the sun and prevent it from escaping back into space. The burning of fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and natural gas for energy production is the largest contributor to greenhouse gas emissions.

Deforestation also plays a major role in climate change. Forests act as natural carbon sinks by absorbing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. When forests are cut down, not only is this carbon absorption reduced, but the stored carbon is also released back into the atmosphere.

Climate change has widespread environmental impacts. Rising global temperatures contribute to melting polar ice caps and glaciers, which leads to rising sea levels. Higher sea levels threaten coastal communities and increase the risk of flooding. Climate change also leads to more frequent extreme weather events such as hurricanes, droughts, and heatwaves.

The effects of climate change extend beyond the environment. Agriculture is heavily affected by changing weather patterns, which can reduce crop yields and threaten food security. Water resources are also impacted as rainfall patterns shift and droughts become more common in certain regions.

Governments and international organizations are working to address climate change through policies aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions. Renewable energy sources such as solar power, wind energy, and hydroelectric power are being promoted as alternatives to fossil fuels.

Mitigating climate change requires global cooperation between governments, industries, and individuals. Transitioning to sustainable energy systems, improving energy efficiency, and protecting natural ecosystems are critical steps in addressing this global challenge.
